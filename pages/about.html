About Us

Our Mission

“Emboldening individuals to improve their social, emotional, psychological, and behavioral functioning through multifaceted evaluation and intervention.”

Teaching Site Statement:

Atlas BHW is a teaching site, indicating that we train practicum students and interns. Much like medical residents in hospitals, they learn by working with actual clients, conducting therapy as well as psychological testing. Their work is closely supervised by licensed clinicians. All trainees are doctoral students who have already obtained their Master's degree in psychology. There is a possibility that you may be assigned to an intern. If you do not feel comfortable working with a student and insist on working with one of the licensed clinicians directly, please let us know, and we will try to accommodate you.

Meet the Staff

Dr. Brandon Williams, PhD, MPH

Dr. Brandon Williams received his PhD in clinical health psychology from Virginia State University and his Master of Public Health (concentration in Health Systems Management) from Tulane University. Dr. Williams completed his clinical psychology internship at Harvard Medical School/Cambridge Health Alliance.

Since 2006, Dr. Williams has worked in diverse clinical settings, including a residential substance abuse treatment facility, university counseling center, community services board, regional jail, state psychiatric hospital, public hospital, juvenile correctional facility, and private practice. He has experience working across the age spectrum, including children, adolescents, and adults.

Dr. Williams works full-time with the Department of Juvenile Justice. He is a part-time therapist in private practice, treating individuals with depression, anxiety, various addictions, suicidal ideation, personality disorders, and bipolar disorder. Dr. Williams specializes in psychological assessments. He is also a certified trauma provider.

Marissa Williams, M.A., LPC, CCTP

Marissa Williams (She/Her) is a Licensed Professional Counselor and Certified Clinical Trauma Provider, with 12 years of experience providing therapy, advocacy and support to children, adolescents and adults. She has experience providing therapy to people with a variety of mental and behavioral health needs to include those experiencing symptoms of depression, anxiety, and adjustment. As a Certified Clinical Trauma Provider, she conducts trauma assessments, and supports children and families through the process of healing from trauma.

Marissa provides therapy to people of all ages 4 and up. She is intentional about creating and maintaining a safe therapeutic space for Black Indigenous People of Color, the LGBTQI Community, and other populations that are traditionally marginalized.

In her spare time, Marissa can be found crafting, trying new foods and spending time with her family.

Amelia Jacokes, LCSW

Amelia is a licensed clinical social worker liscensed in both Virginia and California. She spent her internship years working with dementia patients as well as gaining experience with the chronic pain population. While working in the intensive outpatient program environment, she became familiar working with a wide variety of individuals in need of mental health care ranging from substance use disorders and postpartum PTSD to situational depression and panic disorder. Drawing therapeutic interventions from a broad mix of treatment styles ranging from Dialectical Behavior Therapy and Emotion Focused Therapy to Psychodrama and Mindfulness.

In her private practice she mainly works with adults struggling with depression, anxiety, panic, and trauma-related to either long-standing life occurrences or situational events leading to general malcontent.
